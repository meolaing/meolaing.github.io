Active Diversions

Charles River Canoe and Kayak

Take a canoe on the river that divides Cambridge and Boston. One location in Allston, the other near Kendall Square.

Arnold Arboretum of Harvard University

Large, sprawling park with green hills and pretty trees. This is the second place where we met! Orange line accessible.

Blue Hills Reservation

Hiking with rough terrain just outside of Boston. One side is red line accessible (near Shea Memorial Rink). The other side has a small museum with trail maps (Mass Audobon Trailside Museum. Trail maps available here (pdf).

Museums, Historic Sites, etc.

Harvard Museum of Natural History

This is where we met! A lovely Natural History Museum. If you have the chance to go, definitely check out the glass flowers, which the museum is best known for.

New England Aquarium

Multifloor cylindrical tank with helical ramp traveling around. Wide variety of penguins of different species on the ground level.

Museum of Fine Arts, Boston

Boston's enormous world class fine art museum.

Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum

Museum that began as the private collection of philantrophist and art collector Isabella Stewart Gardner.

Freedom Trail

The Freedom Trail is ai 2.5 mile brick line that passes through Boston and Charlestown passes by many of the city's most famous historic sites. There are a lot of great things to see along the path, but there isn't enough time to look at them in one day. We suggest picking a few that are near each other to visit. Website here.
